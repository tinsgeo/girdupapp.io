How It Works

Simple. Disciplined. Uncompromising.

GirdUp locks the apps you choose — social media, news, streaming, whatever pulls you away — until your Word and prayer are done. The mechanic is intentional. The discipline is real.

01

Wake Up. Apps Lock.

Every morning, GirdUp activates. The apps you selected — social media, news, browsers, whatever you chose — are locked. Not broken. Purposed. A daily reminder that you made a commitment and you're being held to it.

02

Read 15 Minutes of Scripture

The built-in Bible reader tracks your reading time. No shortcuts. No skimming. The Word of God — in full — for 15 uninterrupted minutes. Your mind is being armed.

03

Pray

After reading, GirdUp prompts you to pray. A guided prayer section, prayer journal, or free-form — however you approach God. This is your conversation. Not optional.

04

Unlocked. Armed. Ready.

Your apps unlock. The world gets the version of you that has already been in the Word. Discerning. Grounded. Girded up. Go face the day.

Target Audience

Who Is GirdUp For?

GirdUp is built for the 18–40 believer who is serious about their walk but struggling to hold the line in a world that never stops pulling at their attention.

The Young Believer

Ages 18–28. Navigating college, career, and culture. Digitally native. Consuming more content than any generation before them. Hungry for truth but surrounded by noise. GirdUp gives them a daily anchor before the algorithm does.

The Established Faithful

Ages 28–40. Building families, careers, and lives. Busy. Distracted. Know they should be in the Word but life moves fast. GirdUp removes the decision — the apps they'd waste time on are locked until the Word is done, and the choice is already made for them.

The Discernment-Driven

Believers who feel the weight of cultural deception and want to be equipped to identify false narratives. They're not passive consumers — they want to be watchmen. GirdUp arms them daily with the only truth that never changes.

The Accountability Seeker

Those who've tried devotionals, reading plans, and apps before but needed something with teeth. The app-locking mechanic is the difference. It's not optional. It's a covenant with themselves and with God — enforced on the very apps that used to steal their mornings.
